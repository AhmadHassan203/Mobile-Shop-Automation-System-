# Research and Compliance Notes

**Research date:** 2026-07-13  
**Purpose:** Inform product design; not legal or tax advice.

## 1. Pakistan mobile brand signal

StatCounter's Pakistan mobile-vendor web-usage data for June 2026 listed:

- Infinix: 14%
- Samsung: 13.11%
- Vivo: 12.1%
- Oppo: 9.94%
- Tecno: 9.01%

This is web-usage share, not audited Lahore shop sell-through. Use it as a weak starting signal only. The system's own sales and qualified unmet demand should become the primary source after sufficient data exists.

Source:
https://gs.statcounter.com/vendor-market-share/mobile/pakistan

## 2. PTA and device verification

PTA provides device verification/registration services and a DVS app. The system should store verification result, time and reference rather than claiming to replace the official process.

Sources:
https://www.pta.gov.pk/home
https://dirbs.pta.gov.pk/

PTA also reports local mobile-device manufacturing indicators, reinforcing the need to distinguish official/local/imported product sourcing and warranty status.

Source:
https://www.pta.gov.pk/category/telecom-indicators/171

## 3. Punjab Police e-Gadget

Punjab Police describes e-Gadget as a system intended to help prevent theft and unauthorized mobile-phone sales by enabling status verification and recordkeeping for people in the mobile-phone business.

The product should support a verification/reference workflow for used-device intake, but the exact live process and required fields must be confirmed with the relevant authority before production.

Sources:
https://punjabpolice.gov.pk/e-Gadget
https://punjabpolice.gov.pk/Crime_Prevention_Apps

## 4. FBR digital invoicing

FBR's digital-invoicing FAQ states that electronic invoicing is mandatory for corporate and non-corporate registered persons under the cited 2025 notification, and describes integration through a licensed integrator/PRAL for notified registered persons.

The shop owner must confirm registration status and current applicability with a qualified tax professional. The architecture should keep an integration adapter and invoice/credit-note data required for compliance without blocking the initial internal pilot if the obligation does not apply.

Source:
https://fbr.gov.pk/faqs/173967/173969

## 5. Catalog categories

A current Pakistani electronics retailer publicly lists major mobile brands and categories including Samsung, Apple, Xiaomi, Vivo, Nothing, Oppo, Infinix, Tecno, itel, Realme and Nokia, plus covers, cables, wall chargers, wireless chargers, car chargers, power banks, tablets and smartwatches.

This supports the catalog hierarchy, but not exact Lahore inventory quantities.

Source:
https://www.shophive.com/

## 6. Compliance tasks before production

- Confirm business registration and invoicing obligations.
- Confirm required FBR integration and invoice fields.
- Confirm PTA/DIRBS verification workflow for each device type.
- Confirm current Punjab Police e-Gadget process for used-device purchase/sale.
- Define customer/seller identity-document retention.
- Define warranty/return wording with legal review.
- Define tax-inclusive/exclusive pricing.
- Confirm whether installment/credit sales require additional documentation.
- Keep official process links/configuration editable because rules can change.

## 7. Research limitations

- Public brand shares do not equal local sell-through.
- Marketplace listings can contain misleading or unlawful products.
- Model popularity changes rapidly.
- Exact tax and compliance rules depend on legal/business status.
- The system must not scrape or automate official services without permission and supported integration terms.
